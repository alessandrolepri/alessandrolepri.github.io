About me...

After 7 years in management and customer service, I changed my career into web development. Starting from scratch, my motivation and ambition to become a full-stack developer helped me to overcome difficulties and pressure. I have recently completed a 12 weeks bootcamp with General Assembly and I created 4 projects developing both front-end and back-end. Coding taught me an important lesson: to stay curious and keep a lifelong learning approach to anything I do. This journey boosted my analytical thinking skills and I learned that for solving big problems we have to break them down into minor ones.
For the last 2 and a half years, I have worked in a non-technical role for a different industry. I enjoyed working in a dynamic environment for a company that I was passionate about like wines and spirits.
I was introduced to coding which led me to the Web Development Immersive course at General Assembly. This has fused my love of creativity and logic together which is why I have decided to make this life change.

City Spot (Group project - 4 members)

A group project to design a full-stack React/Express app with a NoSQL database. City Spot is a web application combining 5 APIs, where users can sign up and comment images from their travels all over the World and start interacting with the City-Spot Community and posting the best spot from the city you have been to. All cities in our database are integrated with 3 APIs to provide weekly local forecast, Events and Restaurant recommendation.

Build: React | Node.js | MongoDB | Express | Semantic UI

READ-ME (Group project - 3 members)

What if you don’t even know where to start on your story? READ-ME is an APP for writers and readers that can definitely help. This app is a SQL database written with Python and React that can help you lay the groundwork for your story, from organising all of your novel’s major events. By using Reddit APIs we were able to work on our join tables and relationships in order to provide a great experience for all our users.
